The Best Non-Schengen Countries to Visit

When traveling to the European continent, many backpackers and travelers think that countries in the Schengen area are the only option. However, there are a plethora of countries outside of the Schengen zone that offer unique experiences and attractions that are worth exploring. In this article, we'll take a look at the best non-Schengen countries to visit.

UK and Ireland

The United Kingdom and Ireland are two of the best non-Schengen countries to visit. Both countries are steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. The UK offers world-class museums, such as the British Museum and the National Gallery, as well as iconic landmarks like Buckingham Palace and the Tower of London. Ireland, on the other hand, is known for its stunning landscapes, charming villages, and vibrant cities like Dublin and Galway.

One of the benefits of visiting the UK and Ireland is that they are both English-speaking countries, making it easier for tourists to navigate. The countries also have a well-developed tourist infrastructure, with plenty of accommodation options and transportation links. Visitors to the UK and Ireland can enjoy a range of activities, including hiking, surfing, and exploring the local cuisine.

The Balkans

The Balkans are a region of southeastern Europe that include countries like Bulgaria, Serbia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Croatia (although Croatia joined Schengen in 2023.) For years, the Balkans were a bit of a mystery to travelers, but they have slowly started to gain popularity as a tourist destination. The region offers a unique blend of Eastern European and Mediterranean cultures, with a rich history and stunning natural beauty.

One of the highlights of visiting the Balkans is the food. The region is known for its delicious cuisine, which includes dishes like cevapi (minced meat sausages), burek (meat or cheese-filled pastry), and shopska salad. Visitors can also enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and rafting, as well as explore the region's rich cultural heritage.

Romania and Bulgaria

Romania and Bulgaria are two of the most affordable non-Schengen countries to visit in Eastern Europe. Both countries offer a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, with a range of activities and attractions to suit all interests. Visitors to Romania and Bulgaria can explore the region's rich cultural heritage, including ancient monasteries, fortresses, and castles.

Romania and Bulgaria are also known for their stunning natural beauty, with mountains, forests, and coastlines that offer a range of outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy hiking, skiing, and rafting, as well as explore the region's many caves, gorges, and waterfalls. Both countries also have a well-developed tourist infrastructure, with plenty of accommodation options and transportation links.

Turkey and the Former Soviet Republics

Turkey and the former Soviet republics, including Ukraine, Armenia, and Georgia, are some of the most unique non-Schengen countries to visit in Eastern Europe. Turkey is a cultural melting pot, with a rich history that spans thousands of years. Visitors can explore the ancient city of Istanbul, with its stunning mosques, palaces, and bazaars.

The former Soviet republics, on the other hand, offer a unique insight into the region's communist past. Visitors can explore the many museums and historical sites, including the Kremlin in Moscow and the Hermitage Museum in St. Petersburg. The region is also known for its stunning natural beauty, with mountains, forests, and coastlines that offer a range of outdoor activities.

When planning a trip to non-Schengen countries, it's essential to check the visa requirements for each destination. Some countries offer e-visas, while others require a visa application at the embassy or consulate. It's also a good idea to check the COVID-19 entry requirements for each country, as these may change frequently.

Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or an adventure-seeker, there's something for everyone in the non-Schengen countries. By exploring these destinations, you can gain a unique perspective on the region's rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.
